George Washington

The Founding Father of the United States


February 22, 1732 - December 14, 1799

George Washington, the first President of the United States


Here's a time-line of George Washington's life:

  • 1732 February 22: Born in Popes Creek Plantation, Westmoreland County, Virginia. His father was Augustine "Gus" Washington and his mother was Mary Ball Washington
  • 1749: Appointed official surveyor for Culpeper County, Virginia
  • 1753 French and Indian War: Washington given rank of major and he was sent to assess the strength of the French at Ohio Country Valley
  • 1754: Washington received a commission as a Lieutenant Colonel in the Virginia Regiment
  • 1754: Lieutenant Colonel Washington successfully attacked the French camp near present Jumonville
  • 1754: Lieutenant Colonel Washington built Fort Necessity but was forced to surrender it to overwhelming French and Indian forces
  • 1755 July 9 Battle of the Monongahela: Serving under General Edward Braddock the troops were ambushed and defeated by the French and Indians
  • 1755 October: Governor Dinwiddie appoints Washington as commander in chief of all Virginia forces with the new rank of colonel
  • 1758 Forbes Expedition: Successfully droving the French away from Fort Duquesne
  • 1758: Failed to secure a commission as a British officer and turned to working as a planter and politician
  • 1759 January 6: Married Wealthy widow Mrs. Martha Dandridge Custis. The marriage was childless but the couple raised her two children, John Parke Custis and Martha Parke Custis, from her previous marriage
  • 1769 May: George Mason writes resolutions presented by George Washington to the Virginia House of Burgesses opposing taxation without representation
  • 1774: Elected delegate to the First Continental Congress
  • 1775: By this year Washington owned 6,500 acres at Mount Vernon, with over 100 slaves
  • 1775: April 19 Shots fired at Lexington and Concord where weapons depot destroyed
  • 1775 June 15: Appointed general and commander-in-chief of the new Continental Army
  • 1776 July 4: Signed the Declaration of Independence
  • 1776 September 16: Battle of Harlem Heights where Washington's army repulses British attack
  • 1776 October 28: Battle of White Plains force General Washington to retreat to the west pursued by Cornwallis
  • 1776 December 26: Washington crosses the Delaware River and captures a Hessian force at the Battle of Trenton, New Jersey
  • 1777 September 11: General Washington defeated at the Battle of Brandywine
  • 1778 June 27/28: The Battle of Monmouth occurs in New Jersey as Washington's troops and General. Clinton's troops fight to a standoff
  • 1778 July 8: General Washington sets up headquarters at West Point
  • 1778 July 10: France declares war against Britain
  • 1779 August 14: A peace plan is approved by Congress stipulating independence and British evacuation of America
  • 1781 October 19: The British army surrenders at Yorktown - a devastating effect on the British
  • 1783 February 4: England officially declares an end to hostilities in America
  • 1783 September 3: The Treaty of Paris is signed by the United States and Great Britain
  • 1783 November 2: George Washington delivers farewell address to the army
  • 1783 December 23: Washington resigns his commission as commander-in-chief to the Congress of the Confederation.
  • 1784 January 14: The Treaty of Paris is ratified by Congress and the American Revolutionary War officially ends.
  • 1787 May 25: Elected President of the Constitutional Convention in Philadelphia
  • 1789: Unanimously Elected first President of the United States
  • 1789 April 30: Washington took the oath of office as the first President of the United States in New York City. He was addressed as "Mr. President"
  • 1789 June 1: President Washington signed the first act of Congress concerning the administration of oaths Congress established the Department of Foreign Affairs (now the Department of State)
  • 1791: Congress imposed an excise tax on distilled spirits which led to the Whiskey rebellion
  • 1791 December 15: The Bill of Rights became law. The first 10 amendments to the Constitution of the United States guarantee basic liberties to Americans
  • 1792: Congress established a national mint.
  • 1793: Re-elected President of the United States
  • 1793 April 22: Washington issued the Neutrality Proclamation to keep the United States out of the war between France and Great Britain
  • 1794 August 7: Washington invoked the Militia Law of 1792 to suppress the Pennsylvania farmers who refused to pay the federal whisky tax.
  • 1792 November 19: The Jay Treaty to maintain trade with Great Britain
  • 1796: Published his Farewell Address which was issued as a public letter
  • 1797 March: Washington returned to Mount Vernon
  • 1798 July 4: Commissioned Lieutenant General and Commander in Chief of the new United States Army to serve as a warning to France, with which war seemed imminent.
  • 1799 December 14: Died at Mount Vernon at the age of 67. He arranged for his slaves to be freed in his last will and testament
